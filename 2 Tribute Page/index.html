Biography

Allama Iqbal, was a philosopher, poet, and politician in British India who is considered one of the most important figures in Urdu literature and is often referred to as the "Poet of the East" (Shair-e-Mashriq). He was born on November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, which is now in Pakistan

Early Life

Allama Iqbal belonged to a Kashmiri family that had a history of scholars and poets. His father, Sheikh Noor Muhammad, was a devout man who instilled a love for religion and knowledge in Iqbal from a young age.

Education:

Iqbal received his early education in Sialkot and then went on to study at Government College in Lahore. He excelled in his studies and was known for his exceptional intellect

Further Studies:

Iqbal later went to England to study law, philosophy, and Arabic. He earned a Bachelor of Arts degree from Trinity College, Cambridge, and then pursued a PhD in philosophy from the University of Munich in Germany.

Literary Career

Allama Iqbal was not only a poet but also a philosopher. His poetry is characterized by its deep philosophical insights, addressing themes of self-realization, spirituality, and the individual's relationship with society.
He wrote primarily in Persian and Urdu and is considered one of the most important figures in Persian poetry of the 20th century.

Later Years and Legacy:

Iqbal passed away on April 21, 1938, in Lahore, British India (now in Pakistan). His tomb, known as Mazar-e-Iqbal, is located near the Badshahi Mosque in Lahore and is a place of pilgrimage for admirers of his work.
Allama Iqbal's poetry continues to inspire people around the world, especially in Pakistan, where he is considered the national poet. His philosophical ideas and literary contributions remain influential in various fields, including literature, philosophy, and politics.
